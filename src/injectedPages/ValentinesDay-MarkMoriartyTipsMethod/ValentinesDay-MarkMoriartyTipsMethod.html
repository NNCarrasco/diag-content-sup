CAN WE TEMPT YOU TO SOME EXTRA PROFIT?

There are three ways you can make Baileys part of the occasion and generate some tasty extra revenue. It’s all about timing and your team having the confidence to ask the question…

Mark Moriarty

Diageo’s Head Chef in Residence

Traditionally, January is a quiet month. After the hectic highs of Christmas and New Year, customers are more inclined to stay at home until their bank balances recover.


Valentine’s Day comes at a good time because when mid-February rolls around, they feel ready to indulge themselves again. And, of course, many choose to treat that special someone to a memorable dinner and drinks experience.

Here are some of the ways you can be part of it and help your cashflow feel the love too…

FRONT OF HOUSE

Front of house is instrumental to a great experience. Welcoming your guests with a romantic cocktail alternative to a standard glass of champagne will dial up the romance on arrival.

Love the name

Re-working your drinks menu by placing a romantic twist on well-known cocktail names will create a light-hearted atmosphere that gets everyone in the mood.

Get the look

The visual appearance of your cocktails is vital too and it’s easy to doff your cap to the occasion, with a heart-shaped peach or strawberry on a fruity aperitif or a slice of creamy milk chocolate alongside a decadent digestif.

A little nibble

Serving complimentary bites alongside your cocktails will heighten the experience. Don’t advertise them – the surprise will encourage a sense of added value. Personally, I like to offer petit fours at digestif time!

Create a connection

Closely align your food and drink menus through corresponding flavours and themes. By uniting them, you are encouraging guests to make a mental connection for themselves between cocktails and the overall dining experience.

COCKTAILS TO START

GORDON’S PINK SPRITZ

Valentine’s Day

A sweet concotion of Gordon’s pink gin, lemonade and a sparkle of prosecco to finish.

  • 50ml Gordon’s Premium Pink

  • 75ml Lemonade

  • 50ml Prosecco

  • Half a strawberry and 2 raspberries to garnish

  1. Fill a large glass with ice

  2. Add 50ml Gordons Pink Gin

  3. Top up glass with prosecco and lemonade

  4. Garnish with strawberries to serve

Johnnie & PEACH

Valentine’s Day

The classic American flavour, beloved in iced tea around the world! This visually appealing serve will add a touch of New York style to your drink selection.

  • 50ml Johnnie Walker Black Label

  • 125ml Homemade peach iced tea

  • 10ml lemon juice

  • Peach fan and thyme sprig to garnish

  1. Grab a tall highball glass and fill with ice

  2. Add 50ml Johnnie Walker Black Label.

  3. Add peach iced tea and lemon juice

  4. Add peach and thyme

COCKTAILS TO FINISH

chocolate martini

Valentine’s Day

You deserve a little chocolate love. A chilled swirl of smooth Smirnoff Vanilla flavoured vodka and creamy Baileys Chocolate Luxe topped with chocolate shavings...

  • 25ml Smirnoff Vodka

  • 25ml Baileys Chocolate Luxe

  • 10 ml Chocolate shavings

  1. In a shaker with ice, add Smirnoff Vanilla Vodka and Godiva Chocolate Liqueur

  2. Shake and strain into a martini glass

  3. Garnish with shaved chocolate (optional).

Rum hot Choc

Valentine’s Day

Decadent and delicious, Captain Morgan's Spiced and hot chocolate. What's not to like?

  • 50ml Captain Morgan's Spiced

  • 100ml Hot chocolate

  • Top up with cream / marshmallows or chocolate flake

  1. Add Captain Morgan's Spiced to a mug

  2. Make Hot Chocolate as usual in the mug and heat it up

  3. Garnish with squirty cream, marshmallows or a chocolate flake.

Starter Suggestion

Oyster with lemon, Tabasco and pink peppercorn

  • 10 medium sized oysters, shucked and cleaned

  • Lemon Juice

  • Tabasco

  • Pink Peppercorns

  1. Serve the oyster on salt or ice

  2. Dress with some lemon juice, tabasco and a grind of pink peppercorn

DESSERT SUGGESTION

Serve high quality dark chocolate alongside a coffee macaron as a little something special for your guests at the end of their meal.
