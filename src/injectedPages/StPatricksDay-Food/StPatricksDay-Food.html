traditional irish stew

St. Patrick’s day

A comforting and warming this dish, this is the perfect companion for St. Patrick’s Day.

  • 2 large onions

  • 4 large Carrots

  • ½ stewing steak/mince or lamb

  • 8 large potatoes

  • Salt & pepper (personal preference)

  1. Wash and cut onions into moderate size chunks and add to the pot.

  2. Wash carrots and cut into chunks.

  3. Wash and peel the potatoes then cut each one in half.

  4. Slice the meat into smaller pieces. If using stewing mince roll into meatballs.

  5. Get a large pot and fill it with water. Add the potatoes and meat.

  6. Heat pot until water boils then add carrots and onions.

  7. Keep on a low boil and stir every now and then until vegetables are cooked.

Irish sausage. bacon, onion and potato hotpot

  • 1 pound pork sausage links

  • 1/2 pound smoked bacon, cubed

  • 3 carrots, chopped

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and sliced

  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley

  • 1 cup beef stock

  • 1 1/2 cups Guinness

  • 1/2 cup heavy cream

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).

  2. Place the sausages and bacon into a large skillet over medium heat, and cook until the sausages are browned on both sides, about 10 minutes.

  3. Slice the sausages into large chunks, and set the sausage and bacon aside.

  4. Place a layer of 1/3 of the onions in a 9x13-inch baking dish, followed by a layer of 1/3 of the carrots and sliced potatoes. Arrange 1/3 of sausage and bacon in a layer over the potatoes; sprinkle with salt, pepper, and parsley. Repeat layers twice more.

  5. Warm the beef stock in a saucepan over medium heat, and pour in the stout beer and cream. Pour the sauce over the casserole, and let the sauce fill in the spaces in the layers.

  6. Bake in the preheated oven until the potatoes and vegetables are tender, about 1 1/2 hours.

Guinness Smoked Baby Back Ribs

  • 2 racks spare ribs (per 4 servings)

  • Quality barbecue sauce + extra for dripping

For the Marinade (per 4 servings):

  • 4 Cans Guinness® Original

  • 1 tbsp dried fennel seeds

  • 1 tbsp dried garlic granules

  • 1 tbsp light muscovado sugar

  • 1 tsp fine table salt

  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika

  • pinch cayenne pepper

  • pinch white pepper

  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon (optional)

  1. Make up the dry rub by whizzing the fennel seeds and garlic granules in a spice grinder or a pestle & mortar until they are powdered.

  2. Mix with the rest of the ingredients in a large bowl.

  3. Rub all over the rib racks and set in the fridge for 2 hours, overnight is ideal.

  4. Place the rubbed racks on the grill and leave to cook on griddle until the meat starts to shred away from the bones when you insert a fork.

  5. When they are ready, lightly brush the barbecue sauce over the ribs and place back on the grill for 15 minutes.

  6. Remove from the heat and wrap in tin foil to rest another 15 minutes. Serve warm with a little more barbecue sauce, if you want, and a cold Guinness.

GUINNESS ICE CREAM

  • 500ml double cream

  • 500ml whole milk

  • 1 vanilla pod

  • 4 whole eggs

  • 3 egg yolks

  • 500g caster sugar

  • 1.2 litres Guinness® West Indies Porter

  • 2 tablespoons golden syrup

  1. First make a Guinness West Indies Porter reduction by boiling and then simmering with the golden syrup until the texture thickens.

  2. In a separate pan, bring the cream, milk and vanilla to the boil.

  3. Next, place the 4 whole eggs, 3 egg yolks and caster sugar in a bowl and whisk.

  4. Allow the mixture to cool before adding to the eggs and sugar.

  5. Churn the mixture in an ice cream machine, only half filling the barrel as the mixture rises. Scoop out into a metal container and freeze immediately.
