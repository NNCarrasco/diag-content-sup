Get your freak on

Halloween is around the corner and with this year’s event falling on a Thursday, it’s lining up to be a four-day spooktacular. Here’s our guide to making the most of the third biggest drinking occasion of the year.

Expect more customers than usual

No one wants to spend half the night queueing, so make sure you’ve got extra staff available on the door and at the bar, to get people in and served more efficiently.

Larger serves for big groups

Occasions like Halloween attract bigger crowds, especially at the bar. To speed up service, try different drink formats like pitchers of beer and cocktails, cutting the number of visits to the bar without lowering spend.

ReVAMP your menu

As well as Halloween-themed cocktails, change up your food offering with autumnal flavours and put a spooky twist on some favourite dishes. Creating food and drink pairings can enhance the customer experience and maximise sales.

Entertain your customers
Drive extra footfall to your venue by putting on entertainment for the night and promoting it in advance - anything from a Halloween pub quiz to themed games and competitions will get everyone involved. And whatever you do, make sure your decorate!

Spiced baileys latte

A delicious blend of rich Baileys, coffee and unmistakable autumnal spices to warm the heart.

  • 50ml Baileys Original

  • 20ml espresso

  • 135ml milk

  • Cinnamon stick

  • Nutmeg

  • Whipped cream

  1. Pour 250ml of steaming milk into a disposable cup

  2. Add one espresso shot

  3. Pour in 50ml of Baileys

  4. Add nutmeg, whipped cream and cinnamon stick to serve

Ketel one bloody mary

Lively, crisp flavour and silky softness of Ketel One gives new life to a timeless classic.

  • 50 ml Ketel One

  • 20ml Harveys Bristol Cream

  • 90ml tomato juice

  • Yellow bell pepper

  • 8 drops of Tabasco

  • 4 dashes of Worcestershire sauce

  • Celery stick

  1. Put two chopped rings of yellow bell pepper into a cocktail shaker.

  2. Add 50ml Ketel One

  3. Add 90ml tomato juice

  4. Add 20ml Harveys Bristol Cream

  5. Add 8 drops of Tabasco and 4 dashes of Worcestershire sauce

  6. Dip the rim of a highball glass in salt & pepper and fill with ice

  7. Rock the drink with ice and fine-strain to serve, garnishing it with a celery stick
