MAKE A GREAT IMPRESSION WITH YOUR MENU

It’s quick and simple to spritz up your food offering for Valentine’s Day and capitalise on the year’s fourth biggest consumer occasion.

Special deals

Valentine’s is a couples’ show, so construct a value-based deal around them. How about two mains with two glasses of prosecco for £15 a head? Or two courses and two glasses for £20? Consider it as a lunchtime offer if you’re already busy in the evening.

Themed pairing menu

Create a Valentine’s Day Set Menu with a select number of dishes each with a romantic or sharing twist. Pair each one with a perfectly matched cocktail or offer a complimentary bottle of wine and take pre-bookings.

Dessert Suggestion

Heart shaped churros

Put a Mexican twist on Valentine’s with these heart-shaped Churros and rich chocolate dipping sauce.

  • 250ml water

  • 3 tbsp sugar

  • 1/2 tsp salt

  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

  • 130g all purpose flour

  • 25g butter

  • 2.3l oil (e.g. sunflower)

  • Cinnamon sugar

  • 100g granulated sugar

  • 1-2 tsp ground cinnamon

  • 100g chocolate (milk or dark)

  • 125ml double or whipping cream

  1. Mix the sugar and cinnamon on a large plate and set aside. Line tray with baking paper.

  2. Heat the water, sugar, salt, vanilla and butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to the boil then turn heat off and immediately add all the flour.

  3. Stir the flour with a wooden spoon until the dough forms a ball. Set it aside for a few minutes to cool down a little but pipe while stir warm and pliable.

  4. Fit a large piping bag with a large closed star tip. Spoon the dough into the bag and pipe the hearts on the baking paper.

  5. Heat the oil in a large, deep pot. The oil is ready when it reaches 190C | 375F.

  6. Lower the hearts into the oil using a wooden spoon. Do not fry more than 2 at a time. Flip and fry until golden - about 2-3 minutes on each side.

  7. Drain and then roll in the cinnamon sugar.

  8. To make the chocolate sauce, heat the cream until just boiling then pour it over the chopped chocolate. Let it stand for a couple of minutes then stir until chocolate is melted. Stir in the chilli powder if using. Serve the churros plain or dip them in the chocolate sauce.
