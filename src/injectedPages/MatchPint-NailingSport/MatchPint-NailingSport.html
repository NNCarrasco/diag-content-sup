The Difference between winning and losing: screening live sport

SOUND

90% of sports fans say the live commentary that goes with the game is massively important to them. If you don’t have the sound, it’s almost worth not bothering with the picture. A good quality sound system is vital to creating the right atmosphere. /p>

View

If you’re putting in new screens and deciding where to position them, make sure they’re visible from multiple angles to allow more people to enjoy the game. Factor in issues such as glare from lights and windows and comfortable viewing height.

Atmosphere

The bigger the game, the more lively the atmosphere. Be prepared to let a few of pub rules slide for a short time only and accept there may be more of a clean up job to do afterwards.

Bookings

Watching the game is a social event and big groups might want to book their place.See our top table tips. If you don’t offer this, it may be time to reconsider – even as a one off. You could even encourage advance bookings with a prepaid package deal of food, drink and a seat at the front.

Speed of service

When it comes to the half time break, the only aspect of service fans care about is speed. Pre-poured pints, table service or bottles-only fast-track queues can all help reduce waiting times and get customers back in time for the restart.
