Preparing the bar

Set up an ideal workstation with everything you need for cocktail-making:

  • Napkins - in the bar caddy ready to use
  • Glassware - all popular styles within easy reach
  • Ice - Ice bucket on the bar or next to your glassware
  • Spirits - fully stocked on your back bar or speed rail
  • Mixers - Selection in the chiller and on the back bar
  • Garnish - on the back bar: one as ingredient, one for presentation

TRAINING YOUR STAFF

Making sure your staff are well-trained to create the cocktails on your menu correctly and efficiently is the key to your success in the cocktail arena.

OUR TOP 5 TIPS FOR MAXIMISING PROFITABILITY

  1. Be creative. You might consider a different glassware, seasonal fruits or interesting local ingredients that could add an interesting touch to your cocktail offering.
  2. Less is more. Include only a number of drinks that can be prepared perfectly by the less experienced member of your team during peak hours. You don’t need a huge menu to please everyone. What is really important is to balance flavours and options in different categories.
  3. Train your bar staff to be sales people: Make sure your staff try every cocktail on the list to know how to recommend skillfully to their customers.
  4. Include a group serve. People love to share. Include a sharing serve for 3-4 people in your cocktail list.
  5. Remember to consider some hidden costs like time of preparation, glassware, ice and garnishes. Finally, you need to keep a record on your costs and sales. Be prepared to make tough decisions if one of your creations are not reaching the expected targets.
A well-known bar in Sao Paulo, Brazil experienced a 30% sales increase in cocktails with a very simple action: They replaced their 50 serve cocktail menu with an 8 serve cocktail menu during peak hours. Every bartender was ready to deliver great cocktails and all waiting staff were trained to sell every cocktail on that list.
