Don’t Miss the No and Low Alcohol Trend for Dry January

Low in ABV but high in flavour, offer your customers inspiring non-alcoholic and low-alcohol drinks this Dry January.

Everyone’s enjoyed an indulgent December and the guilt has truly kicked in. As customer looks to lighter choices in the new year, this January widen your range to profit from the demand for no and low alcohol drinks.

52% of customers say non-alcoholic beers have become more socially acceptable in the past two years*

In the summer of 2018, sales of alcohol-free beer rose 58% compared to the same period last year**

Sales of beer with an ABV below 2.8% have risen 381% since 2017***

No- and low-alcoholic beers

There is now a good range of no- and low-alcoholic beers on the market. Coined ‘the beer for when you're off the beers’, Pure Brew Lager is an ultra-low alternative with only 0.5% ABV. Described as a hoppy lager with fruity aromas and a hint of citrus flavour. The secret to Pure Brew maintaining its full flavour and character is in its special strain of yeast which ensure it’s a fully fermented beer.

With Pure Brew we wanted to give people a taste experience comparable to regular beer.

Lead Brewer, John Casey

Longer and lighter options

Seedlip is at the forefront of the alcohol-free spirits market, reflecting customers’ desire for a more balanced lifestyle. Stocked in the best bars and restaurants in the world, this super versatile liquid can create some fantastically tasty adult cocktails such as the Espresso Martino featured below.

The power of pre-mix

Customers can still have exciting choices even if they’re doing Dry January. Want to offer choice without purchasing a full bottle of spirit? Dip your toe in with RTD (ready-to-drink). Perfectly mixed with tonic, these also make great offers to include if you’re signed up to on demand food delivery services such as Uber Eats or Deliveroo. Dip your toe in with Gordon's Ultra Low Alcohol Gin & Tonic RTD's.

Water & nutrients

Consider stocking options like Rejuvenation Water – an adult soft drink using plant-based protein/amino acids, natural fruit juices, hydrating electrolytes and botanical extracts. It provides a different take on non-alcoholic drinks and can be a good basis for a mocktail.

Top tips to tempt customers away from water:

  1. January can be an unprofitable month for publicans. Avoid the post December lull by advertising that you’re on board with Dry January around your venue, on your website and in local gyms.
  2. An alcohol free, healthier January can sometimes mean the only option is a cup of tea or a cola. No wonder many are put off visiting the pub. Break the trend and put together a ‘mocktail menu’ with non-alcoholic versions of your top selling cocktails. See our suggestions here.
  3. Soft drinks are often placed on the bottom shelves of the fridges. Consider rearranging your fridge so soft drinks move to the top for a limited time.
  4. If you’ve bought mainly bottles & soft drinks make sure you put them on a menu as fridges are often overlooked as somewhere to find exciting options.
  5. Spread the word! Advertise your offering, tell your staff about them and try them out so you can sell them well.

Nogroni

Alcohol-free treat

Just like the Negroni, this is a simple combination of 3 ingredients stirred and served over ice. In place of the Classic Gin this uses Seedlip Spice which has a robust, earthy palate.

  • 25ml Seedlip Spice

  • 25ml Aecorn Aperetifs Bitter

  • 25ml Aecorn Aperetifs Aromatic

  • Orange slice or wedge

  1. Add all ingredients to a glass with ice and stir

  2. Garnish with an orange peel

espresso martino

Dry January Favourite

A coffee lover’s dream, the espresso martino blends a smooth espresso shot with Seedlip Spice 94, a guilt-free take on the classic espresso martini cocktail.

  • 50ml Seedlip Spice 94

  • 50ml espresso shot

  • 15ml sugar syrup

  1. Add all ingredients to a cocktail shaker and shake to mix

  2. Strain into a martini glass and garnish with 3 coffee beans

A little advice on ranging

Buying in extra stock without knowing if it’s going to sell is risky but can drive repeat footfall once consumers know you have great choices for those doing ‘dry January’.

  1. Choose low & no alcohol bottled beers and spirits which have a long shelf life to reduce to risk of waste.
  2. Putting a low or no alcohol beer on tap is a great idea, but don’t bulk buy stock so you can monitor and replenish based on demand. It may be a new best-seller that stays on year-round!
  3. Interesting & versatile soft drink options give an always available choice but can also be mixed with alcohol to make interesting cocktails if you wish to boost sales or clear stock.
  4. Think about your outlet type when choosing your non alcoholic range. If your venue is a sports club, gym or you get a lot of custom from nearby sports grounds, you’re likely to need an increased range during January. Soft drinks with added health benefits are also likely to sell well if advertised properly.
