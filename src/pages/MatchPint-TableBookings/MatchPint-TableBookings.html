Get table bookings right and guarantee a good night

The sporting calendar is packed with major events happening around the world. For many fans, the next best thing to being there is watching the action unfold from front row seats in the pub with their mates to one side and a cold beer to the other.

As a venue owner or manager it’s natural to want to make the most of every opportunity and offering a table booking service is one option to consider.

The pros of table booking

Fill your tables

Selling seats in advance means guaranteed customers on the night

Secure payments

Ask for a pre-payment and it’s money in the till before the game even starts

Get the drinks in

You can use advanced demand to get a helpful steer on the stock levels you’ll need

Get on top of the game

It can also help you plan your staff rotas, so if things get busy, you know you’re covered

67% of sports fans say guaranteeing a good seat is either very or fairly important when selecting a pub to watch sport in

As well as the benefits, there are some pitfalls to watch out for that could trip you up if not considered and planned for. Like disgruntled regulars who can’t sit in their favourite spot, empty tables right up till kick-off and booked guests not showing up.

Top tips for getting it right…

  1. Start advertising table availability six weeks in advance of the event to capture the most passionate fans

  2. Stipulate that customers book tables for at least an hour beforehand to avoid empty tables

  3. Retain some tables and space for walk-ins to accommodate small groups and regulars

  4. Consider asking for a deposit or a minimum spend per head to guarantee revenue

  5. Put together a ‘big match package’, e.g. food, drink and seat for £25 to drive further revenue

So the good news is, with a little bit of planning and investment, it’s perfectly possible to drive sales with table bookings without alienating your regular customers. Try it for the next big event and let us know how it goes!
