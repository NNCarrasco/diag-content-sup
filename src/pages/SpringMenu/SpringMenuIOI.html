freshen your menu and clean up this spring

The new season brings light, colour, energy and positivity. Capture it in your menus and see your business go from strength to strength.

Spring gives you the unique opportunity to introduce bright new flavours and ingredients, experiment with new menu designs and create fresh interest and engagement among staff and customers alike.

Seasonal menus see an average of 26% uplift in order value*

60% are more likely to choose a seasonal item from a menu because they look more appetising*

40% say seasonal menu items look more healthy compared to regular ones**

*Restaurant Hospitality Report, **Upserve Restaurant Insider

SPRING INTO ACTION BEHIND THE SCENES

GO LOCAL

Pick up the phone to your local suppliers and talk deals on fruit, veg, bread, meat and fish. You’ll add variety and flavour to your menu, your dishes will look and taste better and you’ll be bang on trend with your customers.

CONTROL COSTS

With no shipping or expensive preserving, products that are local and seasonal should cost less to buy in and be more appealing. 60% of consumers say they’re likely to switch to a brand that is more environmentally friendly than their current one **. Plan with an eye on price, portion size, mix of expensive and inexpensive options and the capacity of your kitchen and staff.

GOOD TO GROW

A vibrant seasonal menu is also a ready-made marketing tool. It’s the perfect reason to talk to customers, invite them in to try new dishes and tempt them with offers – this is what your social media channels were born for! Don't forget to upload a version online in .pdf form so that customers can view from their mobiles with ease.

REV UP THE TEAM

An injection of new dishes and drinks breathes new life into your team, keeping them motivated and engaged. Their passion will be infectious, so encourage them to share your new options with customers every day.

** Global Web Index 2019

SPRING INTO ACTION Front of house

GET THE LOOK

Now’s the time to review your menu for spring. Consider size, print stock and overall design. It’s possible to introduce on-trend elements like fresh, bright colours, florals and illustrations while remaining true to your brand.

DON’T FORGET YOUR GLASSES

A fresh new cocktail line-up should also be in your plans. Think beyond the drink to the glass – eye-catching glassware is a vital part of the serve, creates a genuine wow moment and gets the drink noticed at the bar.

CHERRY ON TOP

Capture the scents and colours of the season in your garnishes. Look past the conventional lime wedge to seasonal fruits and flowers, like cucumber, rose, elderflower, lilac and borage, to add a real flourish to your presentation.

SAVE THE PLANET

Making even small commitments to sustainability can have a great impact on customer perceptions. Paper straws, recycled napkins and switching out plastic for glass can make you part of the world’s biggest conversation

AN EXTRA SPRING IN YOUR STEP

Heighten the sense of the season even further with a simple activity or added experience to make every customer’s visit more memorable and profitable…

CREATIVE KIDS

Family meals revolve around the kids. Keep them happy and the parents will be comfortable staying and spending - especially during half term. Set up a simple craft station, with the accessories they need to create a colourful cherry blossom collage to celebrate spring.

TOP IT OFF

Create a vibrant buffet board of spring-themed garnishes for guests to customise their cocktails. Display seasonal berries, flowers and herbs, with tongs or cocktail sticks available to keep things hygienic.

SPRING COCKTAIL IDEAS

With lots of fresh, seasonal flavours to play with, serve up a new selection of cocktails to liven up your menu.

Johnnie & elderflower

Spring Menu

A summery mix of Johnnie Walker Black Label served long over ice, with elderflower and soda.

  • 50ml Johnnie Walker Black Label

  • 20ml Elderflower cordial

  • Top with soda water

  1. Add all ingredients to a Highball glass filled with ice

  2. Stir to mix

  3. Garnish and serve

Johnnie & ginger fruity

Spring Menu

Enjoy a subtle berry - infusion to enhance Johnnie Walker Black Label’s naturally fruity profile, mixed with ginger and finished with fresh raspberries.

  • 50ml Johnnie Walker Black Label

  • 150ml Ginger ale

  • 3 Raspberries

  1. Add raspberries to a Highball glass and muddle

  2. Fill the glass with ice

  3. Add Johnnie Walker Black Label and ginger ale

  4. Stir to mix, garnish and serve

smirnoff soda orange smash

Spring Menu

Smirnoff No. 21 Vodka, ice cubes, refreshingly crisp soda water, and a big mouth-watering burst of freshly squeezed orange.

  • 25ml Smirnoff No. 21 Vodka

  • 150ml Soda water

  • Freshly squeezed juice of half an orange

  1. Add Smirnoff No.21 Vodka to a Highball glass filled with ice

  2. Top with soda water and squeeze the juice of half an orange into the glass

  3. Garnish and serve

gordon’s pink & tonic

Spring Menu

A Pink twist on the classic G&T, crafted to balance the refreshing taste of Gordon’s with the sweetness of raspberries and strawberries with the tang of redcurrant.

  • 50ml Gordon’s Premium Pink

  • 150ml Tonic water

  1. Add all ingredients to a Copa glass filled with ice

  2. Stir to mix

  3. Garnish and serve
