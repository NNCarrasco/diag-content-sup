GUINNESS, RUGBY AND A BURGER

The Guinness Six Nations offers a great opportunity to drive customers to spend more in your venue. Encourage guests to enjoy food with their pint of Guinness and you’ll maximise dwell time and increase takings.

51% of Six Nations consumers drink Guinness during the tournament, the association of the two is that strong

53% of beer occasions involve a light or full meal: +2ppt vs. PY*

Guinness saw a much bigger uplift in sales over other beers in 2018, a huge 39.1% increase against a non-match weekend**

THE REAL DEAL

Even outsidematch days, offer a burger and a Guinness for, say, £10. Get people in a couple of hours before the match for food and they’ll stay for the game–and continue to buy drinks (with the occasional Guinness Clear, of course).

GUINNESS LOVES FOOD

The flavour of Guinness matches well withburgersbecause the roasted barley in the beer is a similar flavour to the chargrilled elements of the meat. Pair a Hophouse 13 with pizza and you’ve got another winning combination ready for the edge-of-the-seat drama of the game.

CREATE AN ATMOSPHERE

Take advantage of customers’ likeliness to visit on weekends –especially Saturdays. Advertise your food and drink offerings well in advance and decorate your venue for the game. Have bookings for customers wanting food and consider offering those having food priority tables near the screen.
