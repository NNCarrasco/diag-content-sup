QUENCH DEMAND FOR NON-ALCOHOLIC DRINKS

We’resure you’ve noticed the increase in customers arriving atyour pub or restaurant ready to socialise with pals who aren’t on the pints but who still want to be part of the ritual. You’re not alone, in fact Ireland is witnessing a surge in the consumption of non-alcoholic drinks. Make sure you offer a full range of no or low alcohol options to ensureeveryone can take part. And you can still get sales in by keeping margins up!

There are three options around making the most of this trend. Cold soft drinks, hot drinks and low or no alcohol versions of alcoholic drinks.

€4.1 billion in 2017 camefrom sales of non-alcoholic beer and it just keeps rising

Drinkers have been cutting back on alcohol in the last decade or so. Consumption is down 23% since 2007 and that trend is set to continue

57% of people say they’d like to see more availability of non-alcoholic beer, according to a survey carried out by TheTaste and the Irish Brewers Association

COLD SOFT DRINKS

Alongside the usual array of juices, consider stocking premium ready-to-drink offerings and cordials. The no and low crowd still want to be part of the fun and they often don’t mind paying a little extra for something decent.

SOME LIKE IT HOT

Don’t let the high street suck up all your daytime trade. Get in on the act with a fabulous array of coffees (lattes, cappuccinos, espresso...) and find a few tasty syrups and toppings.

LOW AND NO ALTERNATIVES

The trend towards no and low is so pronounced Guinness created Open Gate Pure Brew in response. Get in on thesparkling wine and gin alternatives too toget you some decent free publicity on social media. Lay out a display table of your products with a hashtag and handle for your venue.

DRINK RESPONSIBLY

Support the no and low folkswiththe Guinness Clear ‘Sometimes Less Is More’ campaign. Even if they’re on the pints, drinkers can ease things up with a glass of the old H2O every couple of drinks. We love a bit of responsibledrinking.
