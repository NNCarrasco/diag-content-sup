COCKTAIL CREATIONS

Both guests and fashionistas of LFW will love a cocktail. Create a special menu for the fashion week – if you don’t currently offer cocktails, try to perfect just three different serves. That way you know the team can deliver them easily and you can still offer a wide range.

STRIKE A POSE

Pop pictures of your cocktail creations on social before LFW to attract the fashion-forward crowd (and the Old Fashioned crowd). Make sure your lighting is great (natural light works best) and make the most of free image editing apps like VSCO and Snapseed to ensure your posts are high fashion and top quality.

A LITTLE SOMETHING SOMETHING

Offer non-messy food (no mayo on the Prada, darling) and on-the-go options. Try a Wrap-dress Chicken Salad Wrap, Sashay Away Sandwiches or an LBD (a sartorial take on the BLT).

FROW (Front Row) Fizz

Inspired by New York

A sparkling little something for customers to enjoy between shows. The vodka base is off-set by the fruity flavours in the tonic for a refreshing taste.

Download recipe card

  • 50ml CÎROC Vodka

  • Cucumber and watermelon tonic

  • Cucumber slice

  • Lemon thyme

  • Sage

  • Rosemary

  1. First pour your CÎROC into a long glass, then top with tonic. Garnish with your slice of cucumber, thyme, sage and rosemary.

CÎROC Couture

Inspired by Paris

Try this classic vodka and Chambord mix for a sophisticated cocktail that balances sweet with sour.

Download recipe card

  • 50ml CÎROC Vodka

  • 20ml Chambord

  • 20ml fresh lime juice

  • 15ml sugar syrup

  • 1 dash Peychaud's Bitters

  1. Shake it up and strain into a martini glass.

Hair of the Dog

Inspired by Milan

An ‘Old Fashioned’ wouldn’t cut it with your cutting-edge trend setters so here’s a new take on a favourite.

Download recipe card

  • 50ml Copper Dog

  • 1 dash angostura bitters

  • 1 cube sugar

  • Cherry

  • Slice of orange

  1. Put a sugar cube in a glass and add a dash of angostura bitters

  2. Muddle until dissolved

  3. Fill the glass with ice, top up with the Copper Dog

Dark and Stormy Couture

Inspired by Tokyo

Add a dash of coffee to a Dark and Stormy to keep the fashion-forward awake on their highest heels.

Download recipe card

  • 25ml Captain Morgan Original Spiced Gold rum

  • 25ml Don Julio Blanco tequila

  • Ginger beer

  • Freshly brewed espresso

  1. Fill a highball glass with ice and pour in the rum and tequila. Stir and add the ginger beer. Gradually pour in the espresso and serve.
