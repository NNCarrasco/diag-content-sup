Create aChristmas atmosphere in your venue –it’s easy with our handy guide

Most customers (59%) spend almost an hour longer if the venue has impressive Christmas decorations and displays *

Christmas is the most important time of the year for pubs and bars because customers are inclined to visit more, spend more and stay longer. Ensure your share of season’s greetings with a few tips and tricks which are low on cost but high on returns. Create an atmosphere destined to attract footfall, increase dwell time and make more revenue. Here’s how:

The right decorations

Decor is vital in creating the warm atmosphere in which customers want to linger and spend more. There’s no point sticking up tatty banners. Invest in decorations that reinforce the values which are important to your business.

Tip 1

If you’re small and quirky, do the same with your decorations –add some handmade felt robins to the tree and make a few paper chains.

Tip 2

If your venue is traditional put some bold foliage around fireplaces or doorways using wreaths and garlands. Secure with cable ties.

Tip 3

Set up a Christmas table –ideally near the door –to showcase your Christmas menu, events and promotions

Non alcoholic extras

Nothing creates a warming glow like a freebie, but be careful to think of something that’s of minimal cost to you but can leave a lasting positive impression on the customer. Nail this and they’ll be back, and better yet, will share their experience with friends.

Tip 1

Bake some gingerbread as a side to cocktails or coffee or have some to take on the bar. You could even use icing kits to encourage customers to create their own designs.

Tip 2

If you have a food-led outlet, pop some table decorations out to create a yuletide feel for meals. Either buy some ready-made or do some team building as your staff make them.

Lighting

Locate a part of your venue that could accommodate some homely low lighting. Whether it’s a string of fairy lights or a group of candles, soft lighting creates the intimate and warming feeling that makes customers want to linger. You could also try several lamps each with low wattage rather than fewer more powerful bulbs to keep things cosy.

Tip

Place lights in the corners of spaces to help avoid shadows.

Give it a seasonal twist

While you may already be offering mulled wine and cider, you could try something new like Captain Morgan’s Apple & Ginger Smash with applejuice and ginger beer, served hot or cold.A firm winnerat Christmas markets is Pimm’s Winter. Mix 1 part Pimm’s No. 3 Winter with 3 parts warm apple juice for a warming alternative to this summer favourite.

Tip

Try a classic snowball, eggnog or cinnamon butter rum (try Captain Morgan Original spiced Gold Rum).

Entertainment

Complete the atmosphere with something to occupy customers and increasedwell time on less busy times like Sunday mornings with music or making

Tip 1

Christmas carollers can provide that instant hit of atmosphere and cheer. They don’t need to be professional: a local group collecting for charity will sing for free.

Tip 2

Encourage families to stay by providing craft making stations for children. Hire in a provider or just supply baubles and felt tips and let the kids get creative.

Tip 3

Find a local florist to lead a session and host a wreath-making class. This is an ideal way to boost footfall at less busy times.
